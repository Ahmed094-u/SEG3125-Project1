Periodontics

Periodontics is a specialized field of dentistry that focuses on the health of the gums and the supporting structures of the teeth. This branch of dentistry is essential for diagnosing, preventing, and treating periodontal diseases, such as gingivitis and periodontitis, which can have significant impacts on oral and overall health if left untreated.

Gingivitis is the earliest stage of gum disease, characterized by red, swollen, and bleeding gums. If not addressed, it can progress to periodontitis, a more severe condition that affects the bone and connective tissues supporting the teeth. Periodontitis can lead to tooth loss and has been linked to other systemic health issues, including heart disease and diabetes.

Preventive care is a crucial aspect of periodontics. Regular periodontal check-ups and cleanings help maintain gum health and prevent the progression of disease. Periodontists also provide education on proper oral hygiene practices, such as effective brushing and flossing techniques, and lifestyle modifications to reduce risk factors for gum disease.

By focusing on the health of the gums and supporting structures, periodontics plays a vital role in maintaining overall oral health and preventing tooth loss. Periodontists work closely with general dentists and other specialists to ensure comprehensive care for patients with periodontal concerns.
